Introduction

Hey there, I’m Manya, a Computer Science student with a soft spot for systems that just work and a borderline obsession with making things more efficient. Whether it’s streamlining a deployment, designing a clean dashboard, or figuring out why something broke at 2 AM, I genuinely enjoy solving problems and building tools that make life easier for someone, somewhere.

I’ve worked with Jenkins, Kubernetes (K3s and K3d), Docker, Prometheus, Grafana, OAuth2 Proxy, WildFly, PostgreSQL, Redis, MySQL, Helm, Neo4j, PHP, XAMPP, and a mix of ML and data tools like PyTorch, TensorFlow, scikit-learn, Tableau, and Power BI. I’m always exploring new tools, not just to use them, but to understand how they behave under pressure and how each piece fits into the larger system. I learn best by building, breaking, and rebuilding. I ask a lot of questions (a lot), and I get excited by messy problems that need a thoughtful touch. If there’s a chance to collaborate, experiment, or create something that makes an impact, count me in.

Outside systems work, I’m also formally trained in guitar and music theory, and I enjoy building at the intersection of creativity and structure.

Music

Music has been a long-term discipline for me, not a side hobby. I’ve trained across Indian classical foundations (ragas and melodic structure) and Western notation-based guitar curriculum, and I’ve performed in band and ensemble settings as both a vocalist and guitarist.

Where music meets code

This is where my two worlds meet. I built Harmoneyes — a browser instrument you play with your hands over the webcam, no keyboard or mouse. It maps your finger position to notes and chord qualities and sings them back as layered harmony in real time. Because I trained in both Hindustani classical and Western notation, I built a live Sa Re Ga ↔ A B C toggle so the exact same instrument speaks to a sargam-trained musician and a Western-notation musician at once — down to the komal / teevra swara mapping and a tanpura drone mode.
